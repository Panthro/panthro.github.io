# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Investors and operators evaluating Rafael as a founder — looking for track record, conviction, and credibility before a first meeting or investment decision.

**Secondary:** Senior engineering talent considering working with or for Rafael; conference organizers and media booking speakers or covering energy tech and distributed systems.

All three audiences share one need: they arrive already qualified, are not browsing casually, and need the site to confirm their hypothesis fast — "this person is worth taking seriously."

## Product Purpose

Personal brand hub for Rafael Roman — CTO & Co-founder of Upgrid (Swiss energy marketplace), 20+ year software veteran (N26, Personio, GFT), and international conference speaker (Kafka Summit, KotlinConf, WeAreDevelopers, TEDx).

The site's job is to crystallize professional authority with zero friction: no pitching, no convincing — just evidence. It will expand over time to host articles, book recommendations, and tools, becoming a content destination that compounds the brand over years.

Success = a new visitor can answer "who is this person, why does it matter, and how do I reach them" in under 60 seconds.

## Brand Personality

**Precise. Principled. Rare.**

Voice: direct, never verbose. Tone: confident without performance — the confidence of someone who's shipped real things at real scale, not of someone trying to appear successful. The personality of the person, not the archetype of the role.

## Anti-references

- **Generic developer portfolios:** No skills bars, no scrolling project grids, no "Hello World, I'm Rafael and I love to code" energy. This is not a job application.
- **VC-bro personal branding:** No hype, no thought-leader buzzword soup, no manufactured LinkedIn warmth, no "I'm passionate about" language.
- **Agency or design portfolios:** Not case-study heavy or visually flashy. Rafael is a builder and operator. The site earns authority through content and structure, not visual effects.

## Design Principles

1. **Economy of signal.** Every element must earn its place. Nothing decorative. Ornament is a tell; structure is the brand.
2. **Authority through restraint.** Confidence doesn't explain itself. The site doesn't try to impress — it simply presents. Let career facts and prose do the work.
3. **Typography-led.** Hierarchy, spacing, and type choices carry the brand. Color and motion are tools, not decoration.
4. **Rare over recognizable.** Resist every default that "portfolio sites do this." If the aesthetic is predictable from the category, it's wrong.
5. **Built to compound.** The structure welcomes articles, books, and tools without retrofitting. Sections should feel like they always had room for what comes next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Good defaults. WCAG AA as the practical baseline — solid contrast, keyboard navigable, screen reader compatible. No formal compliance requirement, but don't ship anything that would embarrass a technical audience.
